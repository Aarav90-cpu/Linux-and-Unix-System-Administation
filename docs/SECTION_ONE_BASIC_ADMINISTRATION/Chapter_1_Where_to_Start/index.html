Chapter 1 Where to Start

Welcome to the jungle. We built this guide to fill a very specific void in the vast, terrifying ocean of man pages, outdated blogs, and forum posts from 2008 where the accepted answer is "nvm I fixed it."

First, it’s an orientation guide.

We break down the major administrative systems, explain what all the moving parts do, and show you how they actually talk to each other without catching fire. When there are five different ways to do the same thing, we'll give you the brutally honest pros and cons of the most popular options. Because sometimes, the "best practice" is just a trap.

Second, it’s a quick-reference handbook.

For example, take the ps command (which shows running processes). On Linux, it has over 80 command-line options.

Nobody has time for that.

We cut through the noise and give you the handful of combinations that actually matter to keep your system from flatlining.

Finally, we focus on enterprise servers and networks.

That means serious, professional, "please don't let us go down on Black Friday" system administration. Setting up a single server in your basement is easy. Keeping a massive, distributed, cloud-based platform alive during a viral traffic spike, a network partition, or a targeted attack?

This is where things get weird.

We give you the rules of thumb to recover from absolute disaster, and the architectural choices you'll need as your digital empire grows.

A quick disclaimer:

Are we perfectly objective? Absolutely not. System administration is an art where perfectly reasonable people will fight to the death over the "right" way to do things. We're giving you our highly opinionated, battle-tested takes as raw data. Take what works for your environment, and ignore the rest.
